## Interesting Facts
Nellie Burton Frost was born in September 1859 in Medford, Piscataquis County, Maine, the youngest child of Mark and Catherine (Gibberd) Frost. Her early life was marked by upheaval — her father Mark served in the Civil War and died when Nellie was about seventeen, and her mother Catherine eventually became a patient at the Maine Insane Hospital in Augusta.

Like her older sister Sarah, young Nellie was sent away from Maine to live with relatives or guardians. She was adopted by Benjamin A. Longley and Lydia A. Hodgdon, and by the 1870 census she was living in Cambridge, Massachusetts, at just ten or eleven years old. This early separation from her birth family was likely a consequence of the difficult circumstances at home, but it gave Nellie a new start in the thriving Boston metropolitan area.

Nellie married twice, building a family in Massachusetts. Her first marriage was to William R. Bateman on November 5, 1879, in Belmont, Massachusetts. Together they had three children: George William, May Elizabeth Gervin, and Edward R. After this marriage ended, Nellie married Lincoln Coffin Allen on June 16, 1896, in Cambridge, Massachusetts.

Nellie spent her adult life in the Greater Boston area — the 1900 census places her in Cambridge, and by 1920 she was living in Medford, Massachusetts (coincidentally sharing its name with the Maine town where she was born). She lived to nearly 80 years old, dying on April 6, 1939, in Malden, Middlesex County, Massachusetts.
