## Notes
Earliest documented Hinckley ancestor. Per Geni profile https://www.geni.com/people/John-Hinckley-Hinclyne-of-Lenham/6000000042520391316 . Documented as 'A John de Henclyne of Tenham, Kent, member of the Cinque Ports, assessed for Knighting the Black Prince in 1346, paid 20/- in 1348 for the farm of Gore & Pete and 16/- for the rent of Mellefeld (Tenham) to the steward of the Archbishop of Canterbury.' This Mellefeld property reappears in John 'The Elder's' 1463 will, anchoring the descent across three generations.

## Interesting Facts
Member of the Cinque Ports federation (Tenham was within the influence of the Cinque Ports). The 1346 Black Prince Knighting Aid -- a feudal tax assessed when the future Edward, the Black Prince was knighted -- placed him in the documentary record. Mellefeld stayed in Hinckley hands for at least 117 years (1348-1463/64).
