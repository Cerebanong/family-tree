## Interesting Facts
Beryl was born in September 1944 in Sherborne, Dorset, during the wartime evacuation of London. Her family had been separated due to the bombing of London in WWII. After the war ended, the family reunited and returned to their home in London. Their house was still standing, but many neighboring houses had been bombed, and almost everything from their home had been looted during the war.

Beryl started school at age 4. School supplies were scarce in post-war England — students shared reading books, math books, and pencils. She played field hockey and netball, and participated in track and field on sports days. Growing up, her family never owned a car; they walked to school and took buses or trains everywhere.

An avid reader, Beryl spent much of her free time at the library. She grew up with hardship after the war — food, clothing, and everyday necessities were scarce. She had seven sisters and one brother (William Jr). She wrote a letter to her grandson Axton's class (Mrs. Maloney's class) sharing these childhood memories.
