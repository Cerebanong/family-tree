## Interesting Facts
William H. Frost was born on May 24, 1849, in Perry, Washington County, Maine — a coastal town near the Canadian border, far from where the family later settled. He was the eldest son of Mark and Catherine (Gibberd) Frost, and grew up on the family farm, first in Kilmarnock (1850 census) and then in Medford, Piscataquis County (1860 census). William was just twelve years old when his father Mark left to serve in the Civil War.

As a young man, William struck out on his own, heading north to Aroostook County — Maine's vast agricultural frontier. On March 18, 1874, he married Esther M. Thompson in Woodland, Aroostook County, and the couple settled in Chapman, where William worked as a farmer. They raised five children together: William Leslie, Harriet May, James W., Winfield C., and Cora B. The 1880 census records the family in Chapman, with William working the land in one of Maine's most productive farming regions.

Tragically, William died young. On November 8, 1894, at just 45 years and 6 months of age, he passed away in Bradford, Maine, from heart disease. His death certificate confirms his mother as Catherine, connecting him definitively to the Mark and Catherine Frost family. He was buried at Hill Crest Cemetery in Lagrange, the family's ancestral burial ground, alongside his parents and siblings.

William's death left Esther a widow with young children to raise. His story reflects the hard realities of 19th-century rural Maine — early death, demanding physical labor, and the constant challenge of wresting a living from the northern soil. Yet his legacy endured through his five children, who carried the Frost name into the 20th century.
