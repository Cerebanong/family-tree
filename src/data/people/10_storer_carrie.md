## Interesting Facts
Carrie Wilder Storer was born on August 11, 1857, in Bradford, Maine, and would become Kevin's great-great-grandmother. She grew up in the small Penobscot County community of Bradford, and her middle name "Wilder" likely reflects a family connection on her mother's side.

On June 2, 1878, Carrie married John Lyman Frost in Bradford — an event also noted in the Piscataquis Observer newspaper of June 13, 1878, which recorded the date as June 9. The couple initially settled in Orneville before moving to Bangor, where they would spend the majority of their married life. In Bangor, Carrie and John raised their family and became established members of the community, appearing in census records from 1900 through 1920.

Carrie was widowed when John died on November 29, 1921, but she proved to be remarkably resilient. She continued living in Bangor for nearly three more decades, witnessing the Roaring Twenties, the Great Depression, World War II, and the dawn of the atomic age. Her extraordinary longevity meant she outlived not only her husband by 29 years but also most of her contemporaries.

Carrie Wilder Storer Frost died on October 19, 1950, in Bangor, Penobscot County, Maine, at the remarkable age of 93. She was laid to rest at Hill Crest Cemetery in Lagrange, beside her husband John Lyman Frost and near several other members of the Frost family. Her life spanned from before the Civil War to the mid-20th century — nearly a full century of American transformation.
