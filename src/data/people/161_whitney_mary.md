Lee, Maine, is a small inland town in northern Penobscot County in the Mattanawcook Stream/Mattawamkeag River watershed, incorporated in 1832 and named for Stephen Lee, its proprietor. The town's cultural anchor through the siblings' lifetimes was Lee Academy, a private boarding school founded in 1845 and still operating today; the Academy Cemetery where all three siblings are buried takes its name from the school and sits at the heart of the community. Lee's economy in the late 19th and early 20th centuries was built on farming, small-scale lumbering, and service to the surrounding rural population.
