## Interesting Facts
Mary Elizabeth Mosher was born on February 4, 1919, in Brewer, Penobscot County, Maine, just across the Penobscot River from Bangor. She was the daughter of James Albert Mosher and Elsie E. Frost, and Kevin Frank's maternal grandmother. Mary grew up in Bangor, appearing in census records there in 1920, 1930, and 1940. She had two sisters: Eleanor, who died in infancy, and Barbara Louise.

On June 9, 1941, Mary married Clifton Eugene Whitney in Bangor. Clifton was from Lee, Penobscot County, and had grown up in nearby Winn. Their marriage came just months before the United States entered World War II; Clifton would enlist in the Army in November 1942. The couple had at least two children: Gail Frances Whitney and Spencer Hale Whitney.

After the war, Mary and Clifton relocated to Montpelier, Vermont, where they were established by the 1950 census. Montpelier, the state capital, offered a different pace of life from the industrial cities of Maine. The family put down roots there, and Mary lived in the Montpelier area for over three decades.

Clifton died on November 21, 1979, and Mary survived him by four years. She died on November 4, 1983, in Berlin, Vermont, the town adjacent to Montpelier, at the age of 64. She was buried at Green Mount Cemetery in Montpelier alongside Clifton.
