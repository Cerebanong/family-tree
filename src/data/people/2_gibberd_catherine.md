## Interesting Facts
Catherine E. Gibberd was born around 1830 in Skowhegan, Somerset County, Maine, to an immigrant family — her father was a carpenter born in Germany, and her mother was born in France. The surname Gibberd appeared in various spellings including Gibherd and Gilbert, reflecting the fluid nature of 19th-century record-keeping. Growing up in the mill town of Skowhegan on the Kennebec River, Catherine came of age in a community that was rapidly developing during Maine's early statehood years.

On March 11, 1847, Catherine married Mark Frost in Skowhegan, and the young couple began building their family. They moved to the Kilmarnock area and then to Medford in Piscataquis County, where they were farming by 1860. Catherine bore seven children between roughly 1848 and 1859: Sarah, William, James, Clara, John Lyman, Etta Clarista, and Nellie Burton. Life on a Maine farm was demanding, and the family faced additional hardship when Mark left to serve in the Civil War in 1861.

Catherine's life took a tragic turn at some point after Mark's death in 1877. By the 1880 census she was living in Augusta, and by 1900 she was a patient at the Maine Insane Hospital in Augusta, where she appears in the census that year. The circumstances that led to her institutionalization are lost to history, but she spent many years there. By 1910, at roughly age 80, she had relocated to Bangor, where she was living in Ward 7.

Catherine died on August 23, 1910, in Lagrange, Penobscot County, at the age of 79 or 80. Her death record confirms her Skowhegan birthplace and her age. She was laid to rest at Hill Crest Cemetery in Lagrange alongside her husband Mark, reunited in death in the community where several of their children had settled. Her FindAGrave memorial (#113000115) preserves her memory and provides biographical details about the Frost family.
