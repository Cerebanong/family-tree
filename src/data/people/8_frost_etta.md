## Interesting Facts
Etta Clarista J. Frost was born around 1858 in Maine, the sixth of seven children born to Mark and Catherine (Gibberd) Frost. Known by multiple names throughout her life — Calista, Clarista, and most commonly Etta — she was part of a generation of Frost children who would scatter across New England in search of opportunity.

Etta distinguished herself through education at a time when higher learning for women was still relatively uncommon. She attended and graduated from the Maine Wesleyan Seminary in 1881 — the institution now known as Kents Hill School, one of the oldest coeducational schools in America, founded in 1824. The seminary provided a rigorous education that prepared Etta for a career in teaching.

Following her graduation, Etta took a position as a schoolteacher in Middlefield, Connecticut, venturing far from her Maine roots. It was likely through her Connecticut connections that she met Albert R. Tucker, whom she married in 1883. The couple had one child, Albert Perry Tucker. Etta's journey from a farm girl in rural Piscataquis County to a seminary graduate and schoolteacher in Connecticut represents a remarkable trajectory of self-improvement in the late 19th century.

Etta died in 1920, though the exact date and location are not yet confirmed. Her FindAGrave memorial (#70579066) and FamilySearch record (PID: GS2H-B7Y) preserve her place in the family history. Among the Frost siblings, Etta's path was perhaps the most distinguished academically, and her story illustrates the opportunities that education opened for women in post-Civil War New England.
