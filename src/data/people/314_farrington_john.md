## Notes
FamilySearch PID: LCGX-VCP. Buried Center Lovell Cemetery, Lovell, ME. John was born 1802-02-23 in Chelsea (Suffolk County), Massachusetts and migrated to western Maine by 1825 (his son Stephen B. Farrington was born that year in Lovell, Oxford County). He died 1894-06-19 in Mexico, Oxford County, Maine at age 92 — a long life spanning most of the 19th century, from Jefferson's second term to the cusp of the Gilded Age. He was buried in Center Lovell Cemetery, Lovell, ME, indicating his primary community attachment remained with the Lovell area even though he died in neighboring Mexico (both in Oxford County, ~30 miles apart in Maine's western hills near the New Hampshire border). The migration from coastal Chelsea, MA to frontier Maine in the early 1820s was part of a broader post–War of 1812 wave of settlement into Oxford County, which had been opened to homesteading in the 1780s-1800s. Lovell was incorporated in 1800 by the Massachusetts General Court (Maine was still part of Massachusetts until 1820).
