# Thomas Howes (ID: 648)

## Vital Information
- **Born:** ~1601, Norfolk, England (Morningthorpe tradition; disputed)
- **Died:** 1665-10-18, Yarmouth, Barnstable County, Massachusetts
- **Age at Death:** ~64
- **Occupation:** Farmer, Constable, Deputy to General Court
- **Residence:** Yarmouth (now Dennis), Cape Cod, Massachusetts
- **Military:** Member of Council of War (1658)
- **Immigration:** Arrived ~1637, landing at Salem, MA; moved to Yarmouth ~1638

## Family
- **Father:** Unknown
- **Mother:** Unknown
- **Spouse:** Mary Howes (m. ~1630, England)
  - **Children:** Jeremiah Howes

## Notes
One of the early settlers of Yarmouth, Cape Cod. Appointed Constable 1644, receiver of 'oil of the country' 1652, Deputy to General Court 1652, Member of Council of War 1658. English origin consensus is Norfolk — the Morningthorpe Howes family connection was proposed by 19th-century genealogist JC Howes but has been proven inaccurate; specific parents remain unknown. The Howes monument in Dennis, MA reportedly notes that births, deaths, and marriages of his English ancestors are unknown. Will dated 26 Sep 1665.

## Interesting Facts
His widow Mary later married Governor Thomas Prence of Plymouth Colony as his 4th wife.
