## Interesting Facts
John grew up on a small farm in upstate New York with his family. The farm had a horse, two cows, chickens, ducks, rabbits, dogs, cats, and pigs, and there was always plenty of chores after school — collecting hay, milking the cows, and helping pick vegetables and fruits for his mother to can for winter. The property had a pond where the children went fishing and swimming in the summer, and neighborhood kids would come to play softball or help with the animals.

He started first grade in 1949 at a one-room schoolhouse where one teacher taught grades one through eight. His two sisters Margaret and Joan also attended the same school. The school closed soon after he started, and the children were bused to an elementary school in town. The teachers were very strict and used a paddle for misbehavior. There were no art, music, or PE classes, but they had recess twice a day and a lunch period. Softball was a special favorite at recess. He wrote about these memories in a letter to his grandson Axton's class (Mrs. Maloney's class).
