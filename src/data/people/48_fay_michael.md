## Interesting Facts
Michael L. Fay was born around 1822 in Saint-Armand, Quebec, Canada, in the fertile farmland just north of the Vermont border. He was the son of John Fay, an Irish immigrant born around 1779, and Anna Olmsted, who came from a Connecticut family that had settled near the Quebec border. Michael grew up in a household that straddled cultures: his father had crossed the Atlantic from Ireland, his mother brought New England Protestant roots, and the family lived in French-speaking Quebec while maintaining close ties to Vermont. He had nine siblings, including a brother James (born 1813), and it is notable that Michael and a sibling named Jane appear to have been born the same year, raising the possibility they were twins.

When the Civil War erupted to the south, Michael — by then living in Vermont — answered the Union call despite his Canadian birth. He enlisted as a Private in Company H of the 3rd Vermont Volunteer Infantry, one of the storied Vermont regiments that served in the Eastern Theater. The 3rd Vermont was heavily engaged at battles including Lee's Mill, Golding's Farm, and the Wilderness campaign, though Michael's specific service dates and whether he saw combat remain to be verified. His name appears in the 1890 Census of Union Veterans, confirming his honorable service. The fact that he enlisted at all — as a Canadian-born resident — speaks to his identification with the American cause and the Union.

After the war, Michael married Harriet N. Roberts, a Vermont-born woman, and the couple settled in the Orleans County area of northeastern Vermont, close to the Quebec border he had grown up near. By 1870 they were living in Salem, Orleans County, and by 1880 in Brownington, where Michael is listed in the census as a 'trader' — suggesting he was engaged in the local commerce that linked the Vermont border communities with their Canadian neighbors. The couple had two children: Herbert Alvah Fay (born ~1859) and Annie S. Fay (born ~1866), who would later marry Selden Foss Bickford.

Michael L. Fay died on October 29, 1892, in Barton, Orleans County, Vermont, at approximately age 70. He was buried at the Welcome O. Brown Cemetery in Barton alongside his wife Harriet, who survived him until December 30, 1909. His daughter Annie's marriage to Selden Bickford in 1887 had already connected the Irish-Canadian Fay line to the old Vermont Bickford family, and through their daughter Muriel, Michael's blood would flow forward into the 20th century and eventually into the family line documented in this tree.
