## Interesting Facts
John Lyman Frost was born in September 1855 in Maine, a son of Mark and Catherine (Gibberd) Frost. He is Kevin's great-great-grandfather and a central figure in the family's Penobscot County roots. John grew up in Medford, where the 1860 census records the Frost family, and came of age during the difficult post-Civil War years after his father's service and eventual death in 1877.

On June 2, 1878 (per FamilySearch; June 9, 1878, per the Piscataquis Observer newspaper), John married Carrie Wilder Storer in Bradford, Maine. At the time of his marriage he was living in Orneville, and the newspaper notice describes the wedding. The couple settled first in Orneville (1880 census) before eventually moving to Bangor, Maine's third-largest city, where they would spend the rest of their lives.

In Bangor, John and Carrie established themselves as part of the community. Census records from 1900 through 1920 place the family in Bangor, and by 1900, John's household included his widowed sister Clara R. Hubbard, showing the close bonds among the Frost siblings. John was the family anchor in the Bangor area, maintaining connections to the Lagrange community where his parents were buried.

John Lyman Frost died on November 29, 1921, in Bangor, Penobscot County, at the age of 65 or 66. He was laid to rest at Hill Crest Cemetery in Lagrange, joining his parents Mark and Catherine, his brother William, and his brother James in the family plot. His wife Carrie survived him by nearly three decades, living to the remarkable age of 93. Through their descendants, John and Carrie's line continues as the direct ancestral connection to the present-day family.
