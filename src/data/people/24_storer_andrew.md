## Interesting Facts
Andrew Obed Storer was born on July 25, 1811, in Waldoboro, Lincoln County, Maine — a community whose very name commemorated its German immigrant founders, the families who had crossed the Atlantic in the mid-18th century to settle Broad Bay. His grandfather Christian David Storer had been among those German settlers, and the family had maintained deep roots in Waldoboro through his father Andrew George Storer. Andrew Obed's early childhood was shaped by loss: his father died on July 19, 1831, when Andrew was just 19 years old, leaving behind a widow and seven children. Despite that hardship, Andrew came of age in a family with strong community ties and a work ethic forged by generations of frontier farming.

On October 11, 1838, Andrew married Phoebe Young Butler in Hallowell, Kennebec County — a significant journey from Waldoboro, suggesting the couple may have met through extended family or commercial connections along Maine's coast. Phoebe's father was Captain Sarson Butler (1790–1865) of Hallowell. After their marriage the couple settled in Bradford, Penobscot County, where Andrew would spend the remainder of his life as a farmer. By 1850 they were firmly established in Bradford, raising a family that would eventually number eight children: Edward Moody (1840), Andrew George (1842), Franklin Fletcher (1845), Edmund Alonzo (1847), Thomas Wendell (1850), Delbert Francisco (1852), Ida Catherine (1855), and Carrie (1857).

Andrew Obed farmed in Bradford through the turbulent decades of the mid-19th century — years that brought the expansion of Maine agriculture, the rise of the railroad, and the devastation of the Civil War, which likely took sons or neighbors off to service. He died on September 18, 1867, at the age of 56, leaving Phoebe to raise their youngest children alone. His gravestone at Bradford Corner Cemetery bears the inscription "Andrew O. Storer Died Sept. 18, 1867 Æt. 56" — a simple testament that also confirms his parentage: son of Andrew George and Mary M. (Keizer) Storer.

Phoebe outlived her husband by more than four decades, dying on February 6, 1908, at the remarkable age of 91. By 1900 she had moved to Bangor to live with her son Andrew George Storer, who had himself built a life in that city. Andrew Obed's legacy continued through his many descendants in Penobscot County: the Bradford Corner Cemetery became a gathering place for the extended Storer family, and his children spread across Maine and beyond, carrying forward the German-descended Storer name that had first taken root in Waldoboro nearly a century before Andrew's birth.
