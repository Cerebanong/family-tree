## Notes
Son of Edwin W G Sacre and Edith Mary Ellis. Born during WW1. WikiTree managed by Sarah Black. Woolwich was home to the Royal Arsenal, Britain's principal armaments manufacturing site. During WWI (when Joseph was born), the Arsenal employed ~74,000 workers across 1,300 acres. The borough's population was ~117,000 in the early 1900s. During WWII, Woolwich was heavily bombed; in September 1940, ~32,500 worked at the Arsenal, dropping to ~19,000 after the Blitz raids. Joseph would have been 21 at the outbreak of WWII in 1939.

## Interesting Facts
Born during World War I in December 1917. Woolwich was the birthplace of Arsenal F.C. (originally Dial Square/Royal Arsenal FC, founded 1886 by Arsenal workers).
