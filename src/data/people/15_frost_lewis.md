## Interesting Facts
Lewis Edmund Frost was born on March 4, 1890, in Orneville Township, Piscataquis County, Maine, the fifth child of John Lyman Frost and Carrie Wilder Storer. He grew up in the Bangor area, appearing in the 1900 census in Bangor Ward 1 with his parents and siblings. Lewis was the only son in the family to survive to adulthood, his elder brother John Andrew having died in childhood in 1882.

When the United States entered World War I, Lewis registered for the draft in Penobscot County, Maine, and enlisted on April 29, 1918, at Great Works, Maine. He served until his discharge on June 20, 1919. After the war, Lewis eventually moved to Massachusetts. On June 28, 1922, he married Vera Louise Smith in North Attleboro, Bristol County, Massachusetts. The couple had one child, an infant son born in 1924 who tragically died in infancy.

Lewis lived in the North Attleborough area of Massachusetts through at least the 1930s and 1940 census. When World War II came, he again registered for the draft in 1942 in North Attleboro, though at age 52 he was not called to active service. After his first wife Vera, Lewis married Angelina Cora Moyen (1917-1989), a woman 27 years his junior, in Maine sometime after 1944. They had three children together.

By 1950, Lewis had returned to Maine and was living in Lagrange, Penobscot County. He died on July 12, 1962, in Bangor at the age of 72 and was buried at Hill Crest Cemetery in Lagrange alongside his parents and siblings.
