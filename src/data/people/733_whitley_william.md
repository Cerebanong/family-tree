## Notes
Father of Maud Whitley (726). Per Whitfield One-Name Study Randalholme Generations 12-15, William 'died leaving three daughters' -- Maud was one (she received Randalholme as her portion). His wife was a granddaughter of Robert de Vipont/Vetripont (d. 13 Jul 1371); the Vipont line brought the Cumberland Alston Moor / Kirkhaugh lands into the Whitley family. The 1570s witness testimony refers to him as 'John Whetlay or Whitley' rather than William; the One-Name Study uses 'William de Whitelaw' from compiled sources. Death inferred to be before c.1410 since by that date Maud is married to William Whitfield (725) and the Randalholme inheritance had already passed to her. Wife identified 2026-04-28: Joan (Vipont) de Whitlawe (737), b. before 1346, daughter of Nicholas de Vipont (738) and Elena (Aubeney) de Vipont (739), and granddaughter of Robert de Vipont (1303-1371). Per WikiTree Vipont-28. The Randalholme inheritance descended through Joan, not William.

## Interesting Facts
Through his (unnamed) wife, William transmitted the Randalholme estate -- originally Raynerholme -- to the Whitfield line, which in turn inherited Alston Moor lands tracing back to Robert de Vipont (d. 1371) and the medieval Vipont/Vetripont barony.
