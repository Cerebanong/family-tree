## Interesting Facts
Clara R. Frost was born in August 1853 in Maine, the fourth child and second daughter of Mark and Catherine (Gibberd) Frost. She appears in the 1850 census in Norway, Oxford County, and in the 1860 census in Medford, Piscataquis County, growing up on the family farm.

Around 1870, Clara married Philander L. Hubbard in Fairfield, Somerset County, Maine. Philander was considerably older — born in 1831, he was 22 years Clara's senior. Despite the age difference, the couple built a large family together, welcoming five children: Cora E. (1871–1898), Lenora F. (1873–1930), Wilton Sherman (1876–1950), Wilbur (1876–deceased), and Ernest Mark (1877–deceased). The naming of their youngest son Mark after Clara's father is a touching tribute to the Frost patriarch.

The Hubbard family was mobile, following opportunity across Maine. From Fairfield in Somerset County, they moved to Chapman in Aroostook County (1880 census), where Clara's brother William was also farming. By 1900, Clara was living in Bangor Ward 1, Penobscot County, listed in the census as the "Sister" of household head John L. Frost — her brother John Lyman. The 1900 census records her as age 47, born August 1853, married 30 years, with 5 children born and 3 living, confirming the early deaths of two of her children.

After Philander's death in 1906, Clara continued living in the Penobscot County area. The 1930 census finds her in Bradford, age 75, widowed, living in the household of her son-in-law Fred L. Jones, with son Ernest M. Hubbard also present. Clara is believed to have died between 1930 and 1940, as she does not appear in the 1940 census. She was buried at Williams Cemetery in Bradford, Penobscot County, near her husband Philander.
